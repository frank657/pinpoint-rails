Pinpoint · UX exploration — vertical

Vertical segment + note timelines

Vertical suits the tall right-hand panel: it scrolls naturally and scales to long videos with many notes (where a horizontal track runs out of width). Two takes — a proportional rail (real time-distance) and a compact grouped rail (reading-first). Same data: segment Test segment 0:36–0:44; notes at 0:00, 0:27–0:39, 0:36.

A · Proportional rail

Time flows top→bottom to scale. The segment is a tinted zone on the rail; notes are nodes whose cards land inside or outside it. You feel the real gaps (the 0:00 intro sits far above the segment).

Best for: sensing pacing & density. Trade-off: quiet stretches waste vertical space and busy ones collide — production would compress long idle gaps into a small “⋯ 0:27” spacer and cluster overlapping nodes.

B · Compact grouped rail panel pick

Even spacing, grouped by segment with a colored bracket; gaps shown as a thin “⋯” divider instead of empty space. Reads cleanly and stays dense — ideal for the side panel with lots of notes.

Recommendation: ship B as the side-panel default (dense, readable, scrolls), and optionally offer A as a “timeline” toggle for a pacing overview. Both use the same derived containment rule — a note is in a segment when segment.start ≤ note.start < segment.end — so it’s one data model, two renderings. No schema change.
