Three directions for the study experience

All three use a YouTube-style watch layout — big player on the left, a floating, collapsible notes panel on the right (chat-style, with a pinned capture box). Same feature set, three different aesthetics. Click around: the timeline pins on the scrubber, collapse the panel with ✕, and ⌘K in C.

Historical: these mockups predate a product teardown — features shown here (Courses/Curriculums, AI summary, transcript tabs, spaced-repetition review, cloze) were since removed. The shipped model is flat: Video → Notes + Segments, plus the Position/Technique taxonomy. Kept for the visual direction (the Tatami theme that shipped), not the feature set. See ../ARCHITECTURE.md.

What every direction has to nail
The research kept pointing at the same five jobs — each mockup solves all five, just with different emphasis.

① One-key capture

Note at the current second without pausing flow — point, range, or cloze.

② Click-to-seek

Every timestamp (notes, transcript, chapters) jumps the player to that moment.

③ Review that starts in seconds

The thing Anki gets wrong — due cards, themed queues, instant flip.

④ Momentum

Streak, continue-watching, course %, "8 due today" — the return hooks.

Mockups are static HTML for direction-setting — fonts, color, layout, and interaction feel. They map onto the real Inertia/React pages (videos/Show, review/Index, Dashboard, positions/Index) once you pick a direction (or a hybrid — e.g. B's reading layout for the notebook, C's palette + review-inbox for power use, A's player for focus mode). Sources: Snipd / Recall / LunaNotes (timestamped-notes UX), Mochi / Flashrecall / Space (modern spaced-rep UI), NN/g & LearnDash (instructional-video + focus-mode guidelines).
